\section{Physics Context}
% \section{物理背景}

% TODO: rewrite the 

In a typical hard proton--proton collision, one parton from each proton
participates in the short-distance scattering. At high luminosity and high
collision energy, however, more than one parton pair can undergo hard
scattering in the same proton--proton interaction. Such multiple parton
interactions (MPI), including double parton scattering (DPS) and triple parton
scattering (TPS), probe the transverse spatial distribution and possible
correlations of partons in the proton~\cite{Z48W3VLV,6WUW5N9B,WHQAFHD5}. The
connection between heavy quarkonia and gluon-initiated reactions makes
multi-quarkonium final states particularly valuable in this program. 

% APPROVED

Heavy-quarkonium production in high-energy proton--proton collisions is a central testing ground for quantum chromodynamics (QCD). The heavy-quark mass provides a perturbative scale for the short-distance production of a heavy quark--antiquark pair, while the formation of a color-neutral bound state involves nonperturbative dynamics. This dual nature makes quarkonium measurements sensitive to both perturbative calculations and hadronization models.
~\cite{LANSBERG_QUARKONIUM_REVIEW,NRQCD_BBL1995}.

% TODO: rewrite the heavy flavour quarkonia paragraph to include both bottomonium and charmonium
The \(J/\psi\) meson is especially useful in collider experiments due to its abundance, its narrow decay width, and its clean dimuon decay final states. At the LHC, single-\(J/\psi\), \(J/\psi\)-pair, and other associated-quarkonium measurements have therefore become standard probes of quarkonium production mechanisms. They also provide experimental access to correlations among partons inside the proton, given their dominant production mechanism of gluon-initiated hard scattering, or decay from B-hadron produced in hard scatterings.



\section{Multi-Quarkonium Final States as MPS Probes}
% \section{作为多重部分子散射探针的多重夸克偶素末态}

% TO_APPROVE

Final states containing more than one quarkonium provide a direct way to test
the interplay between single parton scattering (SPS) and multiple parton
scattering (MPS). In \(J/\psi\)-pair production, SPS contributions can be
studied through invariant-mass and angular-correlation observables, while DPS
contributions become important in regions where the two quarkonia are weakly
correlated. This has motivated both theoretical studies of \(J/\psi\)-pair
production in DPS-sensitive phase space and experimental measurements by LHC
experiments~\cite{BORSCHENSKY_KULESZA_2017,CMS_JPSI_PAIR_7TEV,ATLAS_JPSIJPSI_8TEV}.

% TO_APPROVE

The sensitivity increases further for final states with three heavy quarkonia.
Shao and Zhang showed that triple prompt-\(J/\psi\) hadroproduction is a hard
probe of multiple-parton scatterings, because the SPS contribution is
suppressed relative to DPS and TPS components in relevant LHC
conditions~\cite{XREDRBCJ}. The CMS observation of triple-\(J/\psi\) production
then demonstrated experimentally that a rare multi-quarkonium final state can
be reconstructed through six muons and interpreted in terms of SPS, DPS, and
TPS components~\cite{CMS_TRI_JPSI}. These results provide the immediate physics
context for extending the multi-quarkonium program to related final states with
different resonance content and background composition.

% TO_APPROVE

The effective cross section used in many DPS and TPS studies is not a
fundamental constant. Rather, it summarizes, within a simplified factorized
description, information about parton densities, transverse overlap, and
parton--parton correlations. Comparisons across final states are therefore
important: measurements involving jets, electroweak bosons, open heavy flavor,
and quarkonia do not necessarily probe identical parton configurations. A
channel with two \(J/\psi\) mesons and one \(\phi(1020)\) meson can test whether
the qualitative picture established by double- and triple-quarkonium studies
remains consistent when one hidden-charm quarkonium is replaced by a light
strange vector meson.

\section{The \texorpdfstring{\(J/\psi+J/\psi+\phi\)}{J/psi+J/psi+phi} Channel}
% \section{\texorpdfstring{\(J/\psi+J/\psi+\phi\)}{J/psi+J/psi+phi} 道}

% TO_APPROVE

This thesis focuses on the associated production process
\[
  pp \to J/\psi + J/\psi + \phi + X,
\]
with the experimentally relevant decay topology
\[
  J/\psi \to \mu^+\mu^-,
  \qquad
  \phi(1020) \to K^+K^- .
\]
The two \(J/\psi\) candidates provide four charged muons that can be selected
with CMS muon reconstruction and trigger information, while the \(\phi(1020)\)
candidate supplies a narrow \(K^+K^-\) resonance reconstructed from charged
tracks. The Particle Data Group values for the relevant resonance properties
and branching fractions are used as external inputs where needed, without
replacing analysis-specific efficiencies or yields~\cite{PDG2024}.

The \(J/\psi+J/\psi+\phi\) final state is experimentally close to existing
multi-muon quarkonium analyses but is not a simple copy of the triple-\(J/\psi\)
case. Replacing one \(J/\psi\) by a \(\phi\) changes the final-state mass scale,
the charged-track composition, and the combinatorial background. It also brings
track-quality, kaon-assignment, and \(K^+K^-\) mass-window choices into the same
analysis chain as the dimuon vertexing and \(J/\psi\)-candidate selection. The
channel is therefore a useful case study for building a coherent CMS analysis
workflow around a rare associated-production signature.

% TO_APPROVE

From the physics point of view, the channel can be interpreted as a
multi-resonance final state sensitive to SPS and MPS mechanisms. From the
experimental point of view, the main task is to define reproducible candidate
reconstruction, event selection, efficiency correction, and signal-extraction
procedures. No numerical result is assumed in this introduction; final yields,
efficiencies, significances, cross sections, and systematic uncertainties must
come from the analysis inputs documented in later chapters.

\section{Thesis Goals and Scope}
% \section{论文目标与范围}

% TO_APPROVE

The goal of this thesis is to organize the CMS \(J/\psi+J/\psi+\phi\) analysis
into a traceable undergraduate thesis framework. The thesis is written around
the analysis workflow rather than around unconfirmed final numbers. Its scope
is therefore to establish the physics motivation, describe the detector and
samples, document reconstruction and selection choices, and reserve a clear
path for efficiency corrections, signal extraction, systematic uncertainties,
and final interpretation.

% TO_APPROVE

The specific objectives are:

\begin{itemize}
  \item to motivate the \(J/\psi+J/\psi+\phi\) final state as a complementary
  probe of multi-quarkonium and MPS physics;
  \item to connect the channel to public studies of \(J/\psi\)-pair,
  \(\Upsilon(1S)\)-pair, and triple-\(J/\psi\) production without importing
  unpublished CMS conclusions;
  \item to define the reconstructed objects, decay channels, candidate
  combinations, and event-level selections needed for the analysis;
  \item to separate fixed external inputs, such as resonance properties, from
  analysis-dependent quantities, such as efficiencies, fitted signal yields,
  and systematic uncertainties;
  \item to preserve explicit placeholders wherever final analysis outputs are
  still pending.
\end{itemize}

% TO_APPROVE

The thesis does not attempt to provide a new theoretical calculation of the
\(J/\psi+J/\psi+\phi\) production cross section. It also does not archive CMSSW
% TO_APPROVE

analysis code, ROOT ntuples, or private intermediate datasets. Instead, it
records the physics reasoning, the analysis definitions, the final figures and
tables selected for the thesis, and the provenance information needed to make
the written result reviewable.

\section{Thesis Organization}
% \section{论文结构}

% TO_APPROVE

The remainder of the thesis follows the workflow of a CMS experimental
analysis. Chapter~2 reviews the theoretical background for heavy-quarkonium
production, multiple parton scattering, and the relevance of the
\(J/\psi+J/\psi+\phi\) final state. Chapter~3 introduces the CMS detector, the
% TO_APPROVE

data-taking conditions, and the data and simulation samples used in the
analysis. Chapter~4 describes the reconstruction of \(J/\psi\) and \(\phi\)
candidates and the event-selection strategy. Chapter~5 presents the framework
for efficiency corrections, signal extraction, and systematic-uncertainty
evaluation. Chapter~6 is reserved for the final results, cross-checks, and
physics discussion once the approved numerical inputs are available.
Chapter~7 summarizes the analysis and gives the outlook for possible follow-up
studies.
