\chapter{Systematic Uncertainties}
\label{chap:systematics}

\section{Overview}
\label{sec:syst-overview}

The discussion of systematic uncertainties is organised around the individual steps of the analysis chain, rather than as a simple list of source names. The following categories of systematic effects are considered:

\begin{itemize}
  \item uncertainties related to the signal extraction procedure (signal and background mass models, fixed fit parameters, fit range);
  \item uncertainties in the acceptance and efficiency corrections (MC statistical uncertainty, binning effects, muon and kaon reconstruction and identification efficiencies, trigger efficiency, vertexing and event-level selection efficiencies);
  \item uncertainties related to candidate reconstruction and selection (multiple-candidate treatment, best-candidate selection, candidate-matching and migration effects);
  \item uncertainties from sample modelling (signal MC modelling, kinematic reweighting, model dependence of efficiency corrections, pileup modelling);
  \item normalisation uncertainties (integrated luminosity, branching fractions).
\end{itemize}

Each source is propagated to the corrected signal yield and to the differential distributions. Before the final numerical results are determined, this chapter retains the methodological framework and explicit placeholders, without introducing assumed percentage uncertainties.

\subsection{Monte Carlo Statistical Uncertainty}
\label{subsec:syst-mc-stat}

The limited size of the MC samples used to compute the acceptance and efficiency maps introduces a statistical uncertainty in each $(p_{\mathrm{T}}, |y|)$ bin. This uncertainty is propagated to the corrected yield by fluctuating the per-bin correction factors within their Clopper--Pearson binomial uncertainties.

\section{Luminosity and Branching Fraction Uncertainties}
\label{sec:syst-normalization}

\subsection{Integrated Luminosity}
\label{subsec:syst-luminosity}

The integrated luminosity of the data sample is $289.21 \pm 5.97~\text{fb}^{-1}$, corresponding to a relative uncertainty of 2.1\%~\cite{CMS:LUMI-PUB}. This uncertainty is fully correlated across all bins and is applied as a global normalisation uncertainty.

\subsection{Branching Fractions}
\label{subsec:syst-branching-fractions}

The world-average branching fractions $\mathcal{B}(J/\psi \to \mu^+\mu^-) = (5.961 \pm 0.033)\%$ and $\mathcal{B}(\phi \to K^+K^-) = (49.1 \pm 0.5)\%$~\cite{PDG2024} enter the cross-section calculation. Their uncertainties are propagated as fully correlated normalisation uncertainties.

\subsection{Combination Procedure}
\label{subsec:syst-combination-procedure}

The total systematic uncertainty is obtained by adding all individual sources in quadrature, assuming they are uncorrelated. For sources with both correlated and uncorrelated components, the two components are summed separately and then combined.
