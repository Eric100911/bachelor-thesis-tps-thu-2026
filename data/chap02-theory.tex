\section{From Single- to Multiple-Parton Scattering}
% \section{从单部分子散射到多重部分子散射}

% TO_APPROVE

In the usual leading picture of a hard proton--proton collision, one parton from each incoming proton participates in a short-distance partonic subprocess, while the remaining constituents form the beam remnants and the underlying event.
This mechanism is called single-parton scattering (SPS).
It is the default language for perturbative calculations of high-scale final states and for many measurements of parton distribution functions.
At the LHC, however, the large parton luminosity, especially in gluon-rich low-\(x\) regions, makes it possible for two or more parton pairs to undergo separate hard scatterings in the same proton--proton collision.
Such events are collectively referred to as multiple-parton scattering (MPS), with double-parton scattering (DPS) and triple-parton scattering (TPS) as the most commonly discussed cases~\cite{Z48W3VLV,denterriaDoubleTripleNparton2017}.
% 在通常的硬质子--质子碰撞图像中，每个质子各有一个部分子参与短程散射，其余部分子形成束流余迹和底事件。
% 在 LHC 的高部分子亮度下，同一次质子--质子碰撞中可能发生两个或更多独立硬散射，统称为 MPS。

% TO_APPROVE

MPS is not only a correction to event activity.
It probes information about the proton that is not contained in ordinary one-body parton distribution functions.
SPS measurements mainly constrain longitudinal momentum densities, whereas MPS observables are sensitive to the transverse spatial distribution of partons and to correlations in momentum, flavor, color, and spin.
These correlations are nonperturbative QCD inputs and are therefore difficult to predict from first principles.
For this reason, measurements in several final states and at different scattering multiplicities are needed to test whether the simplified factorized picture is sufficient~\cite{Z48W3VLV,denterriaDoubleTripleNparton2017}.

\section{Double, Triple, and General \texorpdfstring{\(n\)}{n}-Parton Scattering}
% \section{双重、三重和一般 \(n\) 重部分子散射}

% TO_APPROVE

For two hard final states \(A\) and \(B\), DPS corresponds to the simultaneous occurrence of two partonic subprocesses in one hadronic collision.
If \(A\) and \(B\) are produced independently, their joint rate is enhanced when the two colliding protons overlap strongly in the transverse plane.
For three hard final states \(A\), \(B\), and \(C\), TPS extends the same idea to three parton--parton interactions.
The formal generalization to \(n\)-parton scattering is useful because it makes the combinatorial and geometric structure explicit, even when only DPS and TPS are experimentally accessible~\cite{denterriaDoubleTripleNparton2017}.

% TO_APPROVE

The important point for this thesis is that a multi-resonance final state can be partitioned in more than one way.
For \(J/\psi+J/\psi+\phi\), SPS would place the complete final state in a single hard scattering.
DPS could produce a pair in one scattering and the remaining resonance in another, for example \((J/\psi+J/\psi)+\phi\) or \((J/\psi+\phi)+J/\psi\).
TPS would instead assign each resonance to a separate hard scattering.
Because the two \(J/\psi\) mesons are identical, the topology classification must avoid double counting while still allowing the experimentally reconstructed candidates to be treated symmetrically.
% 对 \(J/\psi+J/\psi+\phi\) 末态，SPS、DPS 和 TPS 的拓扑划分不同；两个 \(J/\psi\) 全同粒子要求组合计数时避免重复。

\section{Pocket Formulae and Effective Cross Sections}
% \section{口袋公式与有效截面}

% TO_APPROVE

The common phenomenological starting point for MPS is the independent-scattering approximation.
In this approximation, correlations among the separate hard scatterings are neglected and the complicated transverse overlap of partons is summarized by an effective cross section.
For \(n\) hard final states \(A_1,\ldots,A_n\), the corresponding pocket formula may be written as~\cite{denterriaDoubleTripleNparton2017}:
% 在独立散射近似下，多个硬过程之间的关联被忽略，横向重叠几率由有效截面参数吸收。

\begin{equation}
  \label{eq:nps-pocket}
  \sigma_{\mathrm{NPS}}^{A_1+\cdots+A_n}
  =
  \frac{\mathfrak{m}}{n!}
  \frac{\prod_{i=1}^{n}\sigma_{\mathrm{SPS}}^{A_i}}
       {\left(\sigma_{\mathrm{eff},\,\mathrm{NPS}}\right)^{n-1}} .
\end{equation}

% TO_APPROVE

Here \(\mathfrak{m}\) is a combinatorial factor that accounts for identical final states and removes duplicate assignments, and \(\sigma_{\mathrm{eff},\,\mathrm{NPS}}\) has dimensions of area.
In the simplified geometric interpretation, a smaller effective cross section corresponds to a larger probability for several parton pairs to scatter in the same \(pp\) collision.
The parameter should not be read as a fundamental constant.
It absorbs transverse parton profiles, correlations, fiducial definitions, and residual model assumptions into a compact phenomenological quantity.

% TO_APPROVE

For DPS, Eq.~\eqref{eq:nps-pocket} reduces to
\begin{equation}
  \label{eq:dps-pocket}
  \sigma_{\mathrm{DPS}}^{A+B}
  =
  \frac{\mathfrak{m}}{2}
  \frac{\sigma_{\mathrm{SPS}}^{A}\sigma_{\mathrm{SPS}}^{B}}
       {\sigma_{\mathrm{eff},\,\mathrm{DPS}}} .
\end{equation}
% TO_APPROVE

For TPS, it becomes
\begin{equation}
  \label{eq:tps-pocket}
  \sigma_{\mathrm{TPS}}^{A+B+C}
  =
  \frac{\mathfrak{m}}{3!}
  \frac{\sigma_{\mathrm{SPS}}^{A}\sigma_{\mathrm{SPS}}^{B}\sigma_{\mathrm{SPS}}^{C}}
       {\left(\sigma_{\mathrm{eff},\,\mathrm{TPS}}\right)^2} .
\end{equation}
% TO_APPROVE

D'Enterria and Snigirev showed that, for several transverse parton-density profiles, the TPS and DPS effective cross sections are approximately related by~\cite{WHQAFHD5,denterriaDoubleTripleNparton2017}
\begin{equation}
  \label{eq:dps-tps-relation}
  \sigma_{\mathrm{eff},\,\mathrm{TPS}}
  =
  \kappa\,\sigma_{\mathrm{eff},\,\mathrm{DPS}},
  \qquad
  \kappa = 0.82 \pm 0.11 .
\end{equation}
% TO_APPROVE

This relation provides a practical bridge between DPS and TPS interpretations, but it remains conditional on the assumed transverse profiles and on the same independence assumptions used in the pocket formulae.

\section{Assumptions and Limitations}
% \section{假设与局限}

% TO_APPROVE

The pocket formulae are useful because they provide a transparent normalization estimate and a common language for comparing measurements.
Their simplicity also defines their limitations.
They assume that the hard scatterings factorize, that the relevant transverse parton distribution can be represented by a process-independent effective area, and that correlations among partons can either be neglected or absorbed into \(\sigma_{\mathrm{eff}}\).
They also assume that the single-process cross sections entering the formula are evaluated in compatible phase spaces and with consistent definitions of prompt, nonprompt, inclusive, or fiducial production.

% TO_APPROVE

These assumptions are especially delicate for quarkonium final states.
Associated quarkonium production at LHC energies is often dominated by gluon-initiated subprocesses, so it may probe a different partonic population than electroweak-boson-plus-jet or multijet final states.
The SPS component can also be difficult to model, because heavy-quarkonium production involves both a perturbative heavy-quark pair and nonperturbative hadronization into the physical bound state.
If the SPS template is incomplete, the inferred MPS fraction and the extracted \(\sigma_{\mathrm{eff}}\) can be biased.
Consequently, this chapter uses the pocket formulae as an organizing framework, not as a claim that the final state must be described by independent scatterings alone.
% 口袋公式在本文中作为组织语言，而不是独立散射占优的预设结论。

\section{MPS in Multi-Quarkonium Measurements}
% \section{多夸克偶素测量中的 MPS}

% TO_APPROVE

Multi-quarkonium final states are particularly useful for MPS studies because they are heavy, experimentally distinctive, and often gluon dominated.
Measurements of double-\(J/\psi\), double-\(\Upsilon\), and mixed quarkonium final states have shown that SPS and DPS can populate different regions of invariant mass, rapidity separation, and azimuthal separation.
These variables therefore provide handles for separating correlated single-scattering production from less correlated multiple-scattering production~\cite{CMS_JPSI_PAIR_7TEV,ATLAS_JPSIJPSI_8TEV,CMS_YY_8TEV,BORSCHENSKY_KULESZA_2017}.

% TO_APPROVE

Triple-quarkonium production sharpens this motivation.
Shao and Zhang argued that triple prompt-\(J/\psi\) hadroproduction is a hard probe of MPS because the SPS contribution is suppressed relative to DPS and TPS contributions in the relevant LHC environment~\cite{XREDRBCJ}.
CMS subsequently observed triple-\(J/\psi\) meson production in \(13~\mathrm{TeV}\) proton--proton collision data and compared the fiducial cross section with SPS, DPS, and TPS expectations~\cite{CMS_TRI_JPSI}.
The CMS result provides a direct experimental reference point for using triple-heavy-resonance final states to test the factorized MPS picture.
Ascioti's study further emphasizes the broader program of using charmed mesons and charmonia as probes of TPS following the CMS triple-\(J/\psi\) observation~\cite{ascioti_2022_6fw3g-xj153}.
% Shao--Zhang 给出三重 prompt \(J/\psi\) 作为 MPS 探针的理论动机；CMS 三重 \(J/\psi\) 观测提供直接实验参照；Ascioti 讨论了 charm/charmonium TPS 探针的扩展方向。

\section{Implications for \texorpdfstring{\(J/\psi+J/\psi+\phi\)}{J/psi+J/psi+phi}}
% \section{\(J/\psi+J/\psi+\phi\) 的物理含义}

% TO_APPROVE

The process \(pp\to J/\psi+J/\psi+\phi+X\) is a natural extension of the multi-quarkonium MPS program.
It contains two charmonia and one narrow \(s\bar{s}\) vector meson.
The two \(J/\psi\to\mu^+\mu^-\) decays provide a clean multi-muon signature, while \(\phi\to K^+K^-\) adds a narrow hadronic resonance reconstructed from charged tracks.
From the MPS point of view, the final state is interesting because it admits SPS, DPS, and TPS topology assignments, but it is not identical to triple-\(J/\psi\) production: the \(\phi\) changes the final-state mass scale, decay topology, combinatorial background, and possible production mechanisms.

% TO_APPROVE

Within the pocket-formula framework, a TPS interpretation would relate the final-state rate to the production of two \(J/\psi\) mesons and one \(\phi\) meson in three separate scatterings, with the identical \(J/\psi\) pair handled by the proper combinatorial factor.
DPS interpretations would instead combine one associated pair with one single resonance.
For example, one hard scattering may produce \(J/\psi+J/\psi\) while the other produces \(\phi\), or one scattering may produce \(J/\psi+\phi\) while the other produces the remaining \(J/\psi\).
The analysis must therefore keep the topology language explicit when interpreting any observed yield.
No numerical rate, significance, or MPS fraction is assumed at this stage.

% TO_APPROVE

Experimentally, the same final state also imposes constraints on how the theory language can be used.
Trigger requirements, dimuon reconstruction, charged-track reconstruction, candidate arbitration, common-vertex fits, mass-window choices, and efficiency corrections all enter before a fitted signal yield can be translated into a fiducial cross section or an effective-cross-section statement.
For this reason, the role of this chapter is to define the MPS interpretation framework and the relevant assumptions.
The later analysis chapters must supply the selected phase space, efficiency model, signal extraction, and systematic uncertainties before any conclusion about \(\sigma_{\mathrm{eff}}\), DPS, or TPS can be drawn.
